About Me

My name is Rahul Bhosle. By profession I am a Geospatial Developer with a background in Computer Science and GIS. Currently, I am employed by GDR, Inc. where I have been working since August 2013. Here, I contributed to create an online spatial data management engine (VERAS) for public safety agencies and created a cable services fraud visualization tool. Before working for GDR, I was a student at North Carolina State University from where I graduated from the Master of Geospatial Information and Technology program. At NCSU, as my final project I created an online web mapping application to help Wake County Health Services department visualize "food deserts" in Wake County. I also worked on an Eye Tracking research project at the Center for Earth Observation at NCSU.

I live in Berkeley, CA before which I was located in Raleigh, NC and before that I was in Goa, India. This website showcases projects (mainly Geosptatial) that I have worked on in the past few years. Feel free to get in touch if you have any questions.

Access To Healthy Food In Wake County

In effort to promote healthy life habits and prevent chronic disease, Wake County Human Services (WCHS) is seeking data to inform decision makers and service providers about access to healthy food choices in Wake County. Creation of a database and map showing access to healthy foods can help identify gaps in availability. Chronic diseases such as diabetes and heart disease can often be linked to poor access to healthy foods. Data: Data provided by Wake County includes an Excel file of locations (name, street address) for food distribution facilities including farmers market, food pantry, road side stands and community gardens. Other data needed (not currently up to date) include grocery store, convenience store and fast food establishment locations. Census data and potentially public health data was needed in the analysis to define target populations based on demographics. The WCHS work with groups, such as the Inter-Faith Food Shuttle and the Food Bank of Central and Eastern North Carolina, to meet the nutrition needs of communities identified as FOOD DESERTS . The web application tool that was created can help coordinate efforts across groups to provide services to citizens in need.
